Problem-Driven Engineering

This page highlights real production issues I personally identified, analyzed, and resolved while operating large-scale financial and point systems. Rather than reacting only to reported incidents, I proactively investigated system behavior, performance metrics, and data inconsistencies to uncover hidden problems.

Problems

  • Withdrawal fee calculations were applied at a different timing than balance validation, causing user confusion and inconsistent results.
  • Expired point statistics were calculated based on data insertion dates, which did not reflect the actual expiration execution dates.
  • Monthly expiration reports lacked visibility into daily point expiration trends.
  • User point earnings and usage were not traceable, making it impossible to accurately identify where points were earned, how they were spent, or reconcile balances by date.

Solutions

  • Refined withdrawal validation logic to align fee calculation timing with available balance checks.
  • Introduced daily point expiration batches and reporting to replace coarse monthly statistics.
  • Redefined expiration query criteria to reflect the actual expiration execution window.
  • Implemented explicit earned–spent–expired mappings to enable accurate settlement tracking and historical reconciliation.

Impact

  • Improved accuracy and predictability of withdrawal validation and settlement reports.
  • Reduced reconciliation discrepancies when integrating with external settlement systems.
  • Increased internal trust in financial data consistency and auditability.
